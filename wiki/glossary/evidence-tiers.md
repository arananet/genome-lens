---
term: Evidence tiers
title: Evidence tiers (A / B / C)
category: interpreting
---
Every association in genome-lens carries a confidence **tier** and at least one
cited source. Tiers tell you how much weight a finding can bear.

- **Tier A — Clinical-grade.** ClinVar pathogenic/likely-pathogenic with assertion
  criteria, or ClinGen-curated. May be clinically actionable — always confirm with
  a clinical-grade test before acting.
- **Tier B — Replicated association.** Multiple independent GWAS or meta-analyses,
  usually modest effect size. Informational only.
- **Tier C — Limited evidence.** Weak, single-study, or pharmacogenomic nuance.
  Interpret with caution.

A higher tier means *better evidence*, not *bigger effect*. Even Tier A variants
are rarely deterministic.
