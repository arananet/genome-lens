---
term: SNP
title: Single-Nucleotide Polymorphism (SNP)
category: core
---
A **SNP** is a single position in the genome where people commonly differ by one
DNA letter (A, C, G, or T). Most consumer DNA tests *genotype* a fixed panel of
several hundred thousand SNPs rather than reading your whole genome.

A SNP is just a location of common variation — it is **not** automatically
meaningful. genome-lens only highlights SNPs that appear in its curated,
source-cited knowledge base.

See also: [Allele](#allele), [Genotype](#genotype), [rsID](#rsid).
